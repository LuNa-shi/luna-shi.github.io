# Digest Brief Quality

## Shape

- Start with a short overview for scanning.
- Follow with thematic sections that explain what changed, why it matters, and which source supports the claim.
- Give the most important posts detailed treatment, not just one-line summaries.
- Follow the per-post two-paragraph template in `editorial-sop.md`: first paragraph for facts, second paragraph for comment/context synthesis and a direct take.
- Use image/text cards for core items when public media is available.
- Keep each public digest self-contained. Do not publish run coverage, account tier dumps, raw watchlist maintenance notes, local file paths, or command snippets unless the post is explicitly about the pipeline.
- Use edited titles plus summaries for cards. Do not copy long source post text as the title; titles should name the theme or implication.
- Under each topic, split source links into short analytical paragraphs instead of stacking many one-line bullets.

## Prioritization

- Core: frontier AI labs, agent workflows, evaluation, infrastructure, robotics, strong research curators.
- High: important but sparse or narrower sources.
- Medium: useful sources that should enter only when the topic is relevant.
- Low/archive: general news, entertainment, marketing-heavy, or off-topic accounts.

## Editorial Rules

- Separate facts from interpretation.
- Prefer concrete model/product/research names over vague trend labels.
- Explain implications for agent systems, research workflow, robotics, infra, or personal research direction.
- Do not overfit to engagement metrics; use them as a signal, not a ranking rule.
- Preserve enough links that a reader can audit the brief.
- Use coverage counts and account priorities internally for ranking, not as public body content.
- Do not write generic second paragraphs such as "后续需要观察三点". Replace them with a concise view grounded in comments, replies, quote posts, artifact details, or practical implications.
